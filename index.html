CCATT Viewer

Critical Care Air Transportation Team Viewer is a new generation of patient monitor. It visualizes long trajectories physiologic patterns for in-flight assessment. It provides at-a-glance view of clinical data and assists with prioritizing care for multiple patients.

Group View

Support clinical decision-making in an aeromedical environment, which has confined space, noise, vibration, and limited visibility.

Highly distinguishable colors helps clinicians quickly identify patients who need attention.

Unit View

CCATT viewer presents a patient's condition with simple color codes (green, yellow and red for normal, warning, and alert values). In a noisy in-flight environment, this can improve situational awareness. Auxiliary tools assists clinicians to read precise instant vital signs, to zoom into time intervals of interest.

Comparison

In a level 1 shock trauma center, the CCATT viewer displays 230 bed (grouped into 14 clinical units) for up to 72-hour continuous vital sign data. It shows up to 9 vital signs in complete trajectories: shock index, heart rate, systolic blood pressure, oxygen saturation, intracranial pressure, etc., that are routinely monitored for patients with shock, burns, traumatic brain injury, or respiratory failure.
